# EXECUTIVE DATA REVIEW — LOCKED SECTIONS

---

## 1. Hero  
Pattern: Recognition (Symptoms)  
Concept: Control over the data system vs fragmented state  
Output: Recognition of controlled vs fragmented → leads to Executive Problem Patterns  

Allowed Signals:  
- Inconsistent decisions  
- Conflicting interpretations of the same data  
- Lack of trust in reported outcomes  
- Visible activity without alignment  
- Absence of control despite data presence  

Forbidden Signals:  
- Naming the Review  
- Explaining causes  
- Describing structure or system components  
- Any solution or intervention  

CONTENT:
Decisions vary across teams, even when based on the same data.
Different stakeholders arrive at conflicting interpretations, and reported outcomes are questioned rather than relied upon.
Activity is visible across initiatives, but it does not align into a single direction.
Data is present across the organization, yet control over how it informs decisions is not established.
A controlled system is visible through consistency, alignment, and trust.
A fragmented state is visible through variation, contradiction, and uncertainty.

## 2. Definition  
Pattern: Definition (Positioning)  
Concept: Executive Data Review as a distinct intervention  
Output: Clear understanding of what this is (and what it is not)  

Allowed Signals:  
- Executive-level evaluation  
- System-level diagnostic  
- Time-bounded intervention  
- Focus on control over decisions and execution  
- Clear boundary vs audit / advisory  

Forbidden Signals:  
- Symptoms (problems)  
- Causes of failure  
- Detailed scope breakdown  
- Outcomes or results  
- Delivery mechanics  

CONTENT:
The Executive Data Review is a focused, time-bounded executive intervention that evaluates whether data operates as a system of control over decisions and execution.
It establishes whether the organization functions under clear ownership and control, or as a set of disconnected efforts without a unified system.
This is not an audit, not advisory, and not a technical assessment.
It is an executive-level evaluation of the data function as a system of control.

## 3. Audience  
Pattern: Situational (Triggers)  
Concept: When this becomes necessary  
Output: Recognition of situational fit  

Allowed Signals:  
- Signals of misalignment becoming visible  
- Repeated inconsistency in decisions  
- Escalation of uncertainty despite data  
- Leadership questioning reliability of outputs  
- Increasing coordination friction  

Forbidden Signals:  
- Role titles or org charts  
- Structural explanations  
- Root causes  
- Solution framing  
- Industry or size segmentation  

CONTENT:
This becomes necessary when decision-making shows increasing inconsistency despite the presence of data.
When the same data leads to different conclusions.
When reported performance does not align with observed execution.
When confidence in outputs declines as data investment increases.
When coordination requires more effort, yet alignment does not improve.
When priorities shift without a stable basis, and outcomes cannot be clearly explained.

## 4. Assessment Scope  
Pattern: Structure (System Presence)  
Concept: What system is examined  
Output: Understanding of system components and relationships  

Allowed Signals:  
- Decision definition  
- Data-to-decision linkage  
- Control mechanisms  
- Alignment between decision and execution  
- Interdependence of elements  

Forbidden Signals:  
- Why things fail  
- Symptoms or examples  
- Tools, platforms, technologies  
- Step-by-step methodology  
- Outcomes or recommendations  

CONTENT:
The Review examines the data function as a system linking decisions, data, and execution.
It covers how decisions are defined, how data supports those decisions, and how consistency is maintained across their use.
It evaluates how control is established, how ownership is assigned, and how alignment is sustained.
The focus is on the relationships between these elements as a single system.

## 5. Outcome  
Pattern: Causality (Why)  
Concept: Why current efforts fail and where control breaks  
Output: Clarity on root causes and structural failure  

Allowed Signals:  
- Sources of fragmentation  
- Missing or weak control points  
- Misalignment drivers  
- Structural gaps  
- Reasons decisions are inconsistent  

Forbidden Signals:  
- Solutions or fixes  
- Future-state design  
- Engagement continuation  
- General benefits language  
- Repeating symptoms  

CONTENT:
The Review establishes where control within the system is absent, fragmented, or misapplied.
It identifies the structural points where alignment breaks, where ownership is unclear, and where control mechanisms are weak or missing.
It clarifies how separate elements—decision definition, data, and execution—fail to operate as a unified system.
It explains why consistency cannot be maintained and where the system loses control under pressure.

Concept: Decision paths emerging from findings  
Output: Clear choice between controlled vs fragmented continuation

- Consequence-aware choice  

Forbidden Signals:

CONTENT:
Following the Review, a clear decision emerges.
Proceed to establish control across decisions, data, and execution.
Or continue operating under known constraints, with fragmentation and inconsistency remaining.
The choice is explicit and consequence-aware.

Output: Understanding of delivery model

- Defined interaction model  

Forbidden Signals:

The engagement remains bounded, with a clearly defined scope and interaction model.
