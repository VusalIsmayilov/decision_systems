## 1. Design Direction & Visual System

### 1.1 Positioning Statement

Design a boutique data & AI advisory website positioned as an executive-level partner to the CEO and leadership team.

The website must communicate three core elements:

**Executive Clarity** — Visitors should immediately understand that this firm helps organizations define, structure, and execute their entire data function end-to-end, from strategy to delivery. Avoid technical noise. Frame everything from a business and leadership perspective.

**Authority and Control** — The design should feel like a trusted operator is taking ownership of complex, high-risk data initiatives. Emphasize confidence, structure, and reliability. This is not a vendor. This is someone who ensures things work.

**Practical Execution Without Internal Build-up** — Clearly communicate that companies can achieve a full data capability (strategy, architecture, governance, engineering, AI) without hiring and managing large internal teams. The value is orchestration, direction, and accountability.

### 1.2 Visual Baseline

Use **www.dataofis.az** as the visual and structural baseline. Preserve the same overall design language, including typography, color system, and minimal aesthetic approach.

**Required Visual Adjustments:**

- Shift palette toward slightly darker tones and higher contrast usage, creating a more grounded and operational feel
- Apply slightly heavier typography weights, especially in headings and key sections
- Evolve layout toward more structured, system-oriented composition with clearer section boundaries and modular blocks
- Introduce visible system logic: process flows, structured groupings, clearly organized content
- Maintain the same font family, base color palette, and level of visual simplicity

> **IMPORTANT:** The final result must feel like a more execution-focused extension of www.dataofis.az — not a redesign or departure.

### 1.3 Site Structure

Multi-page website with four pages:

1. **Home** — primary conversion engine
2. **Services** — detailed intervention points
3. **About** — founder positioning and principles
4. **Contact** — conversion page

### 1.4 Global Navigation

**Header:** DataOfis Partners (logo/wordmark) | Services | About | `[Schedule Executive Data Review]` (CTA button)

**Footer:** © DataOfis Partners | Baku, Azerbaijan | contact@dataofis.az | LinkedIn | EN / AZ / RU language switcher

### 1.5 Primary CTA (used globally)

**Label:** `Schedule Executive Data Review`

This CTA appears in the header navigation as a button, at the end of key sections, and as the primary action on the Contact page.

### Section 2: Problem Framing

**Section Heading:** A pattern we see across organizations

*Present as 5 statement cards — each with a bold title and 2–3 line explanation. Not a flat list.*

**1. Data exists — but doesn't drive decisions**
Data teams are producing outputs — but decision-making remains unchanged. Insight does not consistently translate into action.

**2. Multiple reports — no single version of truth**

**3. Data initiatives start strong, then quietly disappear**
Projects launch with momentum, then stall, fragment, or lose visibility. Results are partial at best.

**4. Vendors are building critical systems — but ownership remains unclear**

**5. AI is discussed everywhere — but nothing meaningful reaches production**
Conversations about AI are constant, but without foundations in place, nothing moves beyond exploration.

---

### Group 1 — Direction

*Establishing what should be done, why it matters, and in what sequence — before implementation begins.*

#### Data & AI Strategy

- **What it is:** Definition of priorities, sequencing, and investment focus across data and AI.
- **What it addresses:** Organizations often pursue disconnected initiatives without a clear direction, leading to fragmented effort and limited impact.
- **Your role:** We define a structured, business-aligned strategy and ensure it is actionable — not a static document, but a basis for execution.

#### AI Enablement

- **What it is:** Assessment of readiness and definition of practical AI use cases aligned to business value.
- **What it addresses:** AI is discussed widely but rarely reaches production — because prerequisites, foundations, and realistic scoping are missing.
- **Your role:** We identify where AI can deliver measurable outcomes, ensure the necessary foundations and constraints are in place, and guide implementation through to delivery.


---

### Group 2 — Architecture & Design

*Designing how the system works — so it serves the business, not the technology stack.*

#### Data Architecture Design

- **What it is:** Definition of the target data architecture — including warehouse, lake, integrations, and data flows.
- **What it addresses:** Architecture is often designed around vendor capabilities rather than business requirements, creating dependency and limiting flexibility.
- **Your role:** We design architecture that is structured, scalable, and vendor-independent — ensuring the company retains ownership, flexibility, and long-term control.

#### Data Product Definition

- **What it is:** Specification of what is being built, for whom, and to what standard — datasets, reports, APIs, and analytical outputs.
- **What it addresses:** Organizations build dashboards and pipelines without clearly defined consumers, ownership, or purpose — resulting in outputs no one fully trusts or uses.
- **Your role:** We define data products as owned, measurable assets — aligned to specific business needs and the decisions they support.


---

### Group 3 — Organization & Control

*Establishing ownership, accountability, and operational control across the data function.*

#### Data Governance Setup

- **What it is:** Design and implementation of governance structures — roles, responsibilities, quality controls, and operational processes.
- **What it addresses:** Data exists across the organization, but no one owns it, maintains it, or is accountable for its quality and usage.
- **Your role:** We build governance that is embedded into operations — not a policy document, but a working system of accountability and control.

#### Data Function Setup

- **What it is:** Design of the operating model for the data organization — team structure, workflows, and interaction with business units.
- **What it addresses:** Data teams exist but operate reactively, without clear structure, priorities, or connection to business outcomes.
- **Your role:** We design how the data function works as a system — so it operates with clarity, consistency, and measurable impact.

#### Vendor Selection & Management

- **What it is:** Structured evaluation, selection, and oversight of external vendors and implementation partners.
- **What it addresses:** Vendor decisions are often driven by sales relationships rather than substance — leading to misaligned scope, poor delivery, and long-term dependency.
- **Your role:** We ensure the right partners are selected on merit, contracts are clearly defined, and delivery is continuously controlled.


---

### Group 4 — Execution & Adoption

*Leading execution end-to-end — ensuring initiatives deliver results and become part of how the organization operates.*

#### Data Project & Program Management

- **What it is:** Structuring and managing data initiatives as coordinated programs — with clear milestones, dependencies, and accountability.
- **What it addresses:** Data projects frequently drift, delay, or lose alignment between business and technical teams — delivering partial outcomes at best.
- **Your role:** We keep execution on track, connected to business outcomes, and visible to leadership — not just managed as a list of tasks.

#### Organizational Data Transformation

- **What it is:** Embedding data into business processes, management practices, and organizational decision-making.
- **What it addresses:** Data capability is built but not adopted — decisions continue to rely on intuition, manual processes, or individual knowledge.
- **Your role:** We drive adoption across teams and functions — ensuring data becomes operational, not theoretical. We stay until the capability operates independently.

*These components are defined independently — but only deliver value when connected to the broader system.*

## 6. Conversion Logic & Behavioral Notes

*This section provides context for implementation decisions. It explains the reasoning behind the content structure.*

### 6.1 Conversion Model

This is NOT a lead-capture website. The conversion model is:

**visitor → recognition → trust → conversation → engagement**

The goal is to enter a high-quality conversation with the right decision-maker.

### 6.2 Target Audience

Mid-to-large organizations in Azerbaijan where data exists but is fragmented, multiple systems are in place, reporting is inconsistent, and there are ongoing or planned data/AI initiatives.

**Typical Sectors:**
- Banks and financial institutions
- Insurance companies
- Telecom
- Large retail / distribution
- Energy / utilities
- Large government or quasi-government entities

**Key Buyer Personas:**

- **CEO / Deputy CEO** — Feels decisions are slow, inconsistent, or unclear. Wants better control and visibility.
- **CFO / COO** — Needs reliable numbers. Frustrated with conflicting reports.
- **CIO / IT Director** — Overwhelmed with systems and vendors. Lacks business alignment.
- **Business Unit Heads** — Don't trust data. Rely on manual workarounds.

**Critical Condition:**
Ideal client has budget, feels pain, but lacks internal leadership (no strong CDO). If they already have a strong internal data leader, we become redundant.

### 6.3 Strategic Positioning

**We are NOT:** a vendor, a development company, a tool implementer.
**We ARE:** independent, senior, outcome-focused.

**How We Win:**
- **Independence** — We don't build, so we don't have bias
- **Control over complexity** — We sit above vendors, tools, and teams
- **Business-first** — We start from decisions and business needs, not data models
- **Failure prevention** — We make sure data projects don't fail

### 6.4 Entry Point Strategy

The paid diagnostic is present on the website but framed implicitly:

*"Most engagements begin with a focused diagnostic to establish the current state and define priorities."*

No pricing. No duration emphasis. Positioned as "how we typically begin" — not a product to buy.

### 6.5 What the Essence Sells

**Clarity → Structure → Control → Execution → Adoption**

## 7. Technical & Implementation Notes

### 7.1 Technology

Framework and technology choice is at the discretion of the implementer. Recommended: Next.js or similar modern framework with static generation. Must be responsive, fast, and minimal.

### 7.2 Responsive Design

Full mobile responsiveness required. The site must work flawlessly on mobile devices — many executive visitors will access it from phones.

### 7.3 Language Support

English is the primary language for initial build. The architecture must support Azerbaijani and Russian translations to be added later. Include a language switcher (EN / AZ / RU) in the footer from day one.

### 7.4 Form Handling

The contact form should send submissions to email (contact@dataofis.az). Implementation can use any reliable form service or backend handler.

### 7.5 SEO & Performance

- Semantic HTML throughout
- Proper heading hierarchy (H1, H2, H3)
- Meta descriptions for all pages
- Fast load times — no heavy assets, no unnecessary JavaScript
- Open Graph tags for social sharing

### 7.6 Content Principles for Implementation

- No stock photography
- No decorative elements that don't serve communication
- No animations unless they clarify structure (e.g., process flow)
- Typography and spacing carry the design — not graphics
- Every section must have clear visual boundaries
